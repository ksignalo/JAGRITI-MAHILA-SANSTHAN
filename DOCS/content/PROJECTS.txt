
4. Projects & Programs
Projects & Programs
Over the past three decades, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS) has implemented a wide
range of development projects and community-based programs aimed at empowering
women, protecting children, and improving the socio-economic conditions of
marginalized communities. Through collaborations with government agencies, civil
society organizations, and community institutions, the organization has successfully
carried out impactful initiatives in the fields of education, livelihood, child protection, and
community development.
Below are some of the key projects and programs implemented by Mahila Jagriti
Sansthan:
TRYSEM Training Programs
In the early years of its work, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan implemented skill development
initiatives under the TRYSEM (Training of Rural Youth for Self Employment)
program in collaboration with the Government of Bihar. These training programs were
organized in different blocks of Darbhanga district to equip rural women and youth with
vocational skills that could help them generate income and improve their livelihoods.
Through these initiatives, participants received practical training and guidance to
develop employable skills, enabling them to become economically self-reliant and
contribute to their family’s income.
Baal Panchayat Initiative (2002)
In partnership with Bachpan Bachao Andolan, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan facilitated the
formation of Baal Panchayat and Baal Sansad in villages of Darbhanga district in
2002. This initiative aimed to promote child participation and leadership by creating
platforms where children could voice their concerns and participate in discussions
related to education, child rights, and community issues.
The Baal Panchayat initiative also played an important role in raising awareness about
child labour, child rights, and the importance of education, encouraging
communities to protect children and ensure their access to schooling.
Girls March to School Campaign
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively participated in the “Girls March to School” campaign
in collaboration with the Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation and the 100 Million
Campaign. This initiative aimed to promote access to education for girls and encourage
communities to ensure that girls remain in school.
Through community mobilization, awareness programs, and advocacy efforts, the
campaign helped highlight the importance of girls’ education and inspired families to
support the educational aspirations of their daughters.
Skill Development Programs
Skill development has been one of the major focus areas of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan.
The organization has conducted several training programs for women and youth to
enhance their employability and livelihood opportunities.
These programs include training in:
● Cutting and stitching
● Handicrafts and small-scale production
● Jute bag making and eco-friendly products
● Vocational skills for rural youth
Through these training initiatives, the organization has enabled many women and young
people to develop sustainable livelihood opportunities and improve their economic
independence.
COVID-19 Relief Campaign
During the COVID-19 pandemic, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively supported vulnerable
communities affected by the crisis. The organization distributed essential relief materials
including food supplies, masks, sanitizers, and educational materials to families
and children in need.
These relief activities helped provide immediate support to families facing economic
hardships during the pandemic and demonstrated the organization’s commitment to
community welfare during emergencies.
Jute Bag Training Program with NABARD
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized jute bag making training programs for women
from Self-Help Groups (SHGs) in collaboration with NABARD. This initiative aimed to
promote eco-friendly livelihood opportunities while strengthening women’s economic
independence.
Through hands-on training sessions, women learned the techniques of designing and
producing jute bags, enabling them to develop small-scale income-generating activities.
The initiative also promoted the use of environmentally sustainable products and
supported women’s entrepreneurship.
Through these projects and programs, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues to empower
women, protect children’s rights, promote education, and support sustainable
livelihoods in rural and marginalized communities.
