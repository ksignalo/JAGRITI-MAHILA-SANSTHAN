3. Our Work / Focus Areas
Women Empowerment
Empowering women is at the core of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan’s mission. The
organization works to enhance women’s socio-economic status by promoting skills,
leadership, and financial independence.
Our initiatives include:
● Skill Training Programs: Training women in vocational skills to help them
become economically independent.
● Self-Help Group (SHG) Development: Formation and strengthening of women’s
groups to promote savings, financial inclusion, and collective decision-making.
● Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging women to start small businesses and
livelihood activities through guidance, training, and access to financial
opportunities.
These initiatives help women gain confidence, improve their livelihoods, and actively
participate in community development.
Child Protection
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is committed to safeguarding children from exploitation, abuse,
and neglect while promoting their rights, safety, and overall development. The
organization works closely with communities, schools, and partner organizations to
create a protective environment for children.
Our child protection initiatives include:
● Child Labour Eradication: Conducting awareness campaigns and community
mobilization to prevent child labour and encourage families to prioritize
education.
● Baal Panchayat: Promoting child participation through child-led platforms where
children learn democratic values, leadership, and the importance of community
participation.
● Child Rights Awareness: Organizing awareness programs and community
meetings to educate parents, teachers, and communities about child rights and
protection laws.
Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan aims to ensure that every child grows
in a safe environment and has access to education and opportunities for a better future.
Education
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan believes that education is one of the most powerful tools for
breaking the cycle of poverty and empowering children. The organization actively
supports the education of children from marginalized communities, especially those who
face barriers in accessing quality education.
Key initiatives include:
● Free Educational Support Classes: Mahila Jagriti Sansthan provides free
virtual learning support for children from Class 1 to Class 5, with the support
of Adore India, helping them strengthen their basic learning and academic skills.
● Educational Kits Distribution: To encourage school participation and support
children’s learning, the organization distributes education kits including books,
notebooks, pens, and pencils to children from underprivileged communities.
● School Support Programs: The organization also conducts awareness
programs to encourage school enrollment and reduce dropout rates among
children, particularly girls.
Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan strives to ensure that children from
disadvantaged communities receive the educational support they need to build a
brighter future.
Gender Based Violence
Gender-Based Violence Support and Prevention
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is committed to promoting gender equality and supporting
women and girls who are victims of gender-based violence (GBV). The organization
works to create safe spaces and provide assistance to women facing domestic violence,
abuse, and discrimination.
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively helps women survivors by providing guidance,
awareness, and support to access legal and social protection mechanisms. The
organization conducts awareness programs and community discussions to educate
communities about women’s rights, gender equality, and the importance of preventing
violence against women.
Our key initiatives include:
● Support to GBV Survivors: Assisting women who are victims of violence by
providing guidance, counseling, and helping them access legal and social
support services.
● Awareness on Women’s Rights: Conducting awareness programs and
community meetings to promote gender equality and prevent domestic violence
and discrimination.
● Legal Awareness Programs: Educating women about their legal rights and
available support systems to seek justice and protection.
Planned Shelter and Rehabilitation Support
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is also planning to establish a Shelter Home for women
survivors of gender-based violence. The shelter will provide a safe and supportive
environment for women in distress. During their stay, the organization plans to provide
temporary accommodation, food, counseling, and skill development training, enabling
survivors to rebuild their lives with dignity and independence.
Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan aims to support survivors of violence,
promote gender justice, and create communities where women and girls can live free
from fear, discrimination, and violence.
Skill Development & Livelihood
To improve economic opportunities for women and youth, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
conducts various livelihood and vocational training programs.
Key programs include:
● Jute Bag Making Training: Promoting eco-friendly livelihood opportunities for
women.
● Cutting & Stitching Training: Providing tailoring skills to women to support
self-employment.
● Youth Skill Training: Training youth in various skills to enhance their
employability and income opportunities.
Environment & Sustainability
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively promotes environmental awareness and sustainable
development practices within communities.
Our initiatives include:
● Renewable Energy Awareness: Educating communities about sustainable
energy practices and environmental conservation.
● Environmental Campaigns: Organizing awareness programs and community
activities to promote environmental protection.
Disaster Relief & Community Support
During times of crisis, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan works to support affected communities
and provide immediate relief.
Key activities include:
● Flood Relief Campaigns: Providing emergency assistance and support to
flood-affected families in rural areas.
● COVID-19 Relief Support: Distribution of essential supplies such as food,
masks, sanitizers, and educational materials to vulnerable communities during
the pandemic.
