**Short introduction of MJS
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS)** is a grassroots non-governmental organization
established in **1993** by **Mrs. Indira Kumari** with the vision of empowering women
and protecting the rights of children and marginalized communities. Registered
under the **Societies Registration Act, 1860** , the organization has been working
for more than **three decades** to promote social justice, education, livelihood
opportunities, and community development. Since its inception, MJS has focused
on key areas such as **women empowerment, child protection, education,
skill development, environmental awareness, and community health**.
Through training programs, awareness campaigns, and grassroots mobilization,
the organization has helped thousands of women achieve economic
independence and supported children in accessing education and protection
from exploitation. Over the years, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan has collaborated with
several government and civil society organizations including **NABARD, Bachpan
Bachao Andolan, Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation, and Nehru Yuva
Kendra** , implementing impactful programs across multiple districts of Bihar. With


its community-driven approach and long-standing experience, MJS continues to
work towards building an **equitable, empowered, and inclusive society**.

2.2 Our Journey
The journey of **Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS)** began in **1993** , when **Mrs. Indira
Kumari** , along with a group of dedicated colleagues, founded the organization with a
vision to bring meaningful change in the lives of women and marginalized communities.
At a time when rural women had limited access to education, livelihood opportunities,
and social participation, the founders recognized the urgent need for a grassroots
platform that would empower women and protect the rights of children.
The organization started its work in the rural areas of **Darbhanga and Madhubani
districts of Bihar** , focusing primarily on **women’s empowerment and child
education**. In the early years, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan concentrated on understanding
the challenges faced by marginalized communities and mobilizing local people to
actively participate in the development process. Through community meetings,
awareness campaigns, and grassroots initiatives, the organization began addressing
critical issues such as child labour, lack of education, gender inequality, and limited
livelihood opportunities.
As the organization gained trust and support from the community, its activities gradually
expanded. Mahila Jagriti Sansthan initiated programs related to **skill development,
health awareness, sanitation, environmental protection, and community rights**


**advocacy**. Women were encouraged to form groups, participate in training programs,
and develop income-generating activities that strengthened their economic
independence and social confidence.
Over the years, the impact of these initiatives led to a steady expansion of the
organization’s work beyond its initial operational areas. Today, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
has implemented various development programs across several districts of Bihar
including **Darbhanga, Madhubani, Saharsa, Supaul, Muzaffarpur, Motihari, Bhojpur,
Buxar, Nawada, and Patna**. Through partnerships with government agencies, civil
society organizations, and community institutions, the organization has reached
thousands of women, children, and families in rural and marginalized communities.
Over the past **three decades** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan has grown from a small
grassroots initiative into a recognized organization working for **women’s
empowerment, child protection, education, and sustainable community
development**. Despite challenges, the organization has remained committed to its core
belief that real change begins at the community level.
Today, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues its journey with the same spirit and dedication,
working tirelessly to empower women, safeguard children’s rights, and build stronger
and more inclusive communities across Bihar.

###### Our Journey: Key Milestones

1993 — Foundation of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan was founded by **Mrs. Indira Kumari** along with a group of
dedicated social workers with the vision of empowering women and promoting child
education among marginalized communities in rural Bihar.
1996–1998 — Early Skill Development Initiatives
The organization began conducting vocational training programs under
government-supported initiatives such as **TRYSEM and DWCRA** , providing skill
development opportunities to rural women and youth in Darbhanga district.


2000 — Promotion of Girls’ Empowerment
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized **Balika Mela programs** in collaboration with
government agencies to promote girls’ education, confidence, and participation in social
and educational activities.
2002 — Child Rights and Baal Panchayat Initiative
In collaboration with **Bachpan Bachao Andolan** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan facilitated the
formation of **Baal Panchayat and Baal Sansad** in villages of Darbhanga, encouraging
children to participate in community decision-making and raising awareness against
child labour.
2008–2012 — Education Support Programs
The organization implemented educational initiatives under the **Bihar Education
Project** , establishing learning centers (Utpreran Kendra) to support children’s education
and encourage school enrollment, particularly for girls.
2014–2015 — Environmental Awareness Campaigns
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan participated in the **National Environmental Awareness
Campaign (NEAC)** supported by the Ministry of Environment, Government of India,
organizing workshops and awareness programs on environmental protection and
sustainability.
2015 — Youth Skill Development Programs
In partnership with **Centum Skill India** , the organization conducted skill development
training programs for youth, enhancing their employability and livelihood opportunities.
2017 — Flood Relief Campaign


Mahila Jagriti Sansthan carried out relief and support activities in flood-affected villages
of Darbhanga, providing essential supplies and assistance to vulnerable families.
2019 — Girls’ Education and Child Protection Campaigns
In collaboration with **Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation and Bachpan Bachao
Andolan** , the organization conducted awareness campaigns such as **“Girls March to
School”** and legal awareness programs on child protection.
2020 — COVID-19 Community Support
During the COVID-19 pandemic, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan distributed essential supplies
including food, masks, sanitizers, and educational materials to vulnerable families and
children.
2020 — Women Skill Development Programs
With support from **Nehru Yuva Kendra** , the organization provided **cutting and
stitching training** to rural women, helping them develop livelihood skills.
2023 — Sustainable Livelihood Programs
In collaboration with **NABARD** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized **jute bag making
training programs** for Self-Help Group (SHG) women, promoting eco-friendly
entrepreneurship and sustainable income opportunities.
Today — Continuing the Mission
With over **30 years of grassroots experience** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues to
empower women, protect children’s rights, promote education, and support sustainable
livelihoods across multiple districts of Bihar.

3. Our Work / Focus Areas

### Women Empowerment

Empowering women is at the core of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan’s mission. The
organization works to enhance women’s socio-economic status by promoting skills,
leadership, and financial independence.
Our initiatives include:
● **Skill Training Programs:** Training women in vocational skills to help them
become economically independent.
● **Self-Help Group (SHG) Development:** Formation and strengthening of women’s
groups to promote savings, financial inclusion, and collective decision-making.
● **Entrepreneurship Support:** Encouraging women to start small businesses and
livelihood activities through guidance, training, and access to financial
opportunities.
These initiatives help women gain confidence, improve their livelihoods, and actively
participate in community development.

### Child Protection

Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is committed to safeguarding children from exploitation, abuse,
and neglect while promoting their rights, safety, and overall development. The
organization works closely with communities, schools, and partner organizations to
create a protective environment for children.
Our child protection initiatives include:
● **Child Labour Eradication:** Conducting awareness campaigns and community
mobilization to prevent child labour and encourage families to prioritize
education.
● **Baal Panchayat:** Promoting child participation through child-led platforms where
children learn democratic values, leadership, and the importance of community
participation.
● **Child Rights Awareness:** Organizing awareness programs and community
meetings to educate parents, teachers, and communities about child rights and
protection laws.


Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan aims to ensure that every child grows
in a safe environment and has access to education and opportunities for a better future.

#### Education

Mahila Jagriti Sansthan believes that education is one of the most powerful tools for
breaking the cycle of poverty and empowering children. The organization actively
supports the education of children from marginalized communities, especially those who
face barriers in accessing quality education.
Key initiatives include:
● **Free Educational Support Classes:** Mahila Jagriti Sansthan provides **free
virtual learning support for children from Class 1 to Class 5** , with the support
of **Adore India** , helping them strengthen their basic learning and academic skills.
● **Educational Kits Distribution:** To encourage school participation and support
children’s learning, the organization distributes **education kits including books,
notebooks, pens, and pencils** to children from underprivileged communities.
● **School Support Programs:** The organization also conducts awareness
programs to encourage school enrollment and reduce dropout rates among
children, particularly girls.
Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan strives to ensure that children from
disadvantaged communities receive the educational support they need to build a
brighter future.

##### Gender Based Violence

Gender-Based Violence Support and Prevention
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is committed to promoting gender equality and supporting
women and girls who are victims of gender-based violence (GBV). The organization
works to create safe spaces and provide assistance to women facing domestic violence,
abuse, and discrimination.
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively helps women survivors by providing guidance,
awareness, and support to access legal and social protection mechanisms. The


organization conducts awareness programs and community discussions to educate
communities about women’s rights, gender equality, and the importance of preventing
violence against women.
Our key initiatives include:
● Support to GBV Survivors: Assisting women who are victims of violence by
providing guidance, counseling, and helping them access legal and social
support services.
● Awareness on Women’s Rights: Conducting awareness programs and
community meetings to promote gender equality and prevent domestic violence
and discrimination.
● Legal Awareness Programs: Educating women about their legal rights and
available support systems to seek justice and protection.

###### Planned Shelter and Rehabilitation Support

Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is also planning to establish a Shelter Home for women
survivors of gender-based violence. The shelter will provide a safe and supportive
environment for women in distress. During their stay, the organization plans to provide
temporary accommodation, food, counseling, and skill development training, enabling
survivors to rebuild their lives with dignity and independence.
Through these initiatives, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan aims to support survivors of violence,
promote gender justice, and create communities where women and girls can live free
from fear, discrimination, and violence.

##### Skill Development & Livelihood

To improve economic opportunities for women and youth, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
conducts various livelihood and vocational training programs.
Key programs include:
● **Jute Bag Making Training:** Promoting eco-friendly livelihood opportunities for
women.
● **Cutting & Stitching Training:** Providing tailoring skills to women to support
self-employment.
● **Youth Skill Training:** Training youth in various skills to enhance their
employability and income opportunities.


### Environment & Sustainability

Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively promotes environmental awareness and sustainable
development practices within communities.
Our initiatives include:
● **Renewable Energy Awareness:** Educating communities about sustainable
energy practices and environmental conservation.
● **Environmental Campaigns:** Organizing awareness programs and community
activities to promote environmental protection.

##### Disaster Relief & Community Support

During times of crisis, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan works to support affected communities
and provide immediate relief.
Key activities include:
● **Flood Relief Campaigns:** Providing emergency assistance and support to
flood-affected families in rural areas.
● **COVID-19 Relief Support:** Distribution of essential supplies such as food,
masks, sanitizers, and educational materials to vulnerable communities during
the pandemic.

4. Projects & Programs

## Projects & Programs

Over the past three decades, **Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS)** has implemented a wide
range of development projects and community-based programs aimed at empowering
women, protecting children, and improving the socio-economic conditions of
marginalized communities. Through collaborations with government agencies, civil
society organizations, and community institutions, the organization has successfully


carried out impactful initiatives in the fields of education, livelihood, child protection, and
community development.
Below are some of the key projects and programs implemented by Mahila Jagriti
Sansthan:
**TRYSEM Training Programs**
In the early years of its work, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan implemented skill development
initiatives under the **TRYSEM (Training of Rural Youth for Self Employment)
program** in collaboration with the Government of Bihar. These training programs were
organized in different blocks of Darbhanga district to equip rural women and youth with
vocational skills that could help them generate income and improve their livelihoods.
Through these initiatives, participants received practical training and guidance to
develop employable skills, enabling them to become economically self-reliant and
contribute to their family’s income.
**Baal Panchayat Initiative (2002)**
In partnership with **Bachpan Bachao Andolan** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan facilitated the
formation of **Baal Panchayat and Baal Sansad** in villages of Darbhanga district in

2002. This initiative aimed to promote child participation and leadership by creating
platforms where children could voice their concerns and participate in discussions
related to education, child rights, and community issues.
The Baal Panchayat initiative also played an important role in raising awareness about
**child labour, child rights, and the importance of education** , encouraging
communities to protect children and ensure their access to schooling.
**Girls March to School Campaign**
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively participated in the **“Girls March to School” campaign**
in collaboration with the **Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation and the 100 Million
Campaign**. This initiative aimed to promote access to education for girls and encourage
communities to ensure that girls remain in school.
Through community mobilization, awareness programs, and advocacy efforts, the
campaign helped highlight the importance of girls’ education and inspired families to
support the educational aspirations of their daughters.


**Skill Development Programs**
Skill development has been one of the major focus areas of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan.
The organization has conducted several training programs for women and youth to
enhance their employability and livelihood opportunities.
These programs include training in:
● Cutting and stitching
● Handicrafts and small-scale production
● Jute bag making and eco-friendly products
● Vocational skills for rural youth
Through these training initiatives, the organization has enabled many women and young
people to develop sustainable livelihood opportunities and improve their economic
independence.

###### COVID-19 Relief Campaign

During the **COVID-19 pandemic** , Mahila Jagriti Sansthan actively supported vulnerable
communities affected by the crisis. The organization distributed essential relief materials
including **food supplies, masks, sanitizers, and educational materials** to families
and children in need.
These relief activities helped provide immediate support to families facing economic
hardships during the pandemic and demonstrated the organization’s commitment to
community welfare during emergencies.

###### Jute Bag Training Program with NABARD

Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized **jute bag making training programs** for women
from Self-Help Groups (SHGs) in collaboration with **NABARD**. This initiative aimed to
promote eco-friendly livelihood opportunities while strengthening women’s economic
independence.
Through hands-on training sessions, women learned the techniques of designing and
producing jute bags, enabling them to develop small-scale income-generating activities.
The initiative also promoted the use of environmentally sustainable products and
supported women’s entrepreneurship.


Through these projects and programs, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues to empower
women, protect children’s rights, promote education, and support sustainable
livelihoods in rural and marginalized communities.
