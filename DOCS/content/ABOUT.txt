
2.1 About the Organization
Brief introduction of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan.
The organization was established in 1993 and works for women empowerment, child
education, human rights and community development.
2.2 Our Journey
The journey of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS) began in 1993, when Mrs. Indira
Kumari, along with a group of dedicated colleagues, founded the organization with a
vision to bring meaningful change in the lives of women and marginalized communities.
At a time when rural women had limited access to education, livelihood opportunities,
and social participation, the founders recognized the urgent need for a grassroots
platform that would empower women and protect the rights of children.
The organization started its work in the rural areas of Darbhanga and Madhubani
districts of Bihar, focusing primarily on women’s empowerment and child
education. In the early years, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan concentrated on understanding
the challenges faced by marginalized communities and mobilizing local people to
actively participate in the development process. Through community meetings,
awareness campaigns, and grassroots initiatives, the organization began addressing
critical issues such as child labour, lack of education, gender inequality, and limited
livelihood opportunities.
As the organization gained trust and support from the community, its activities gradually
expanded. Mahila Jagriti Sansthan initiated programs related to skill development,
health awareness, sanitation, environmental protection, and community rights
advocacy. Women were encouraged to form groups, participate in training programs,
and develop income-generating activities that strengthened their economic
independence and social confidence.
Over the years, the impact of these initiatives led to a steady expansion of the
organization’s work beyond its initial operational areas. Today, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
has implemented various development programs across several districts of Bihar
including Darbhanga, Madhubani, Saharsa, Supaul, Muzaffarpur, Motihari, Bhojpur,
Buxar, Nawada, and Patna. Through partnerships with government agencies, civil
society organizations, and community institutions, the organization has reached
thousands of women, children, and families in rural and marginalized communities.
Over the past three decades, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan has grown from a small
grassroots initiative into a recognized organization working for women’s
empowerment, child protection, education, and sustainable community
development. Despite challenges, the organization has remained committed to its core
belief that real change begins at the community level.
Today, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues its journey with the same spirit and dedication,
working tirelessly to empower women, safeguard children’s rights, and build stronger
and more inclusive communities across Bihar.
Our Journey: Key Milestones
1993 — Foundation of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan was founded by Mrs. Indira Kumari along with a group of
dedicated social workers with the vision of empowering women and promoting child
education among marginalized communities in rural Bihar.
1996–1998 — Early Skill Development Initiatives
The organization began conducting vocational training programs under
government-supported initiatives such as TRYSEM and DWCRA, providing skill
development opportunities to rural women and youth in Darbhanga district.
2000 — Promotion of Girls’ Empowerment
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized Balika Mela programs in collaboration with
government agencies to promote girls’ education, confidence, and participation in social
and educational activities.
2002 — Child Rights and Baal Panchayat Initiative
In collaboration with Bachpan Bachao Andolan, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan facilitated the
formation of Baal Panchayat and Baal Sansad in villages of Darbhanga, encouraging
children to participate in community decision-making and raising awareness against
child labour.
2008–2012 — Education Support Programs
The organization implemented educational initiatives under the Bihar Education
Project, establishing learning centers (Utpreran Kendra) to support children’s education
and encourage school enrollment, particularly for girls.
2014–2015 — Environmental Awareness Campaigns
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan participated in the National Environmental Awareness
Campaign (NEAC) supported by the Ministry of Environment, Government of India,
organizing workshops and awareness programs on environmental protection and
sustainability.
2015 — Youth Skill Development Programs
In partnership with Centum Skill India, the organization conducted skill development
training programs for youth, enhancing their employability and livelihood opportunities.
2017 — Flood Relief Campaign
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan carried out relief and support activities in flood-affected villages
of Darbhanga, providing essential supplies and assistance to vulnerable families.
2019 — Girls’ Education and Child Protection Campaigns
In collaboration with Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation and Bachpan Bachao
Andolan, the organization conducted awareness campaigns such as “Girls March to
School” and legal awareness programs on child protection.
2020 — COVID-19 Community Support
During the COVID-19 pandemic, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan distributed essential supplies
including food, masks, sanitizers, and educational materials to vulnerable families and
children.
2020 — Women Skill Development Programs
With support from Nehru Yuva Kendra, the organization provided cutting and
stitching training to rural women, helping them develop livelihood skills.
2023 — Sustainable Livelihood Programs
In collaboration with NABARD, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan organized jute bag making
training programs for Self-Help Group (SHG) women, promoting eco-friendly
entrepreneurship and sustainable income opportunities.
Today — Continuing the Mission
With over 30 years of grassroots experience, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan continues to
empower women, protect children’s rights, promote education, and support sustainable
livelihoods across multiple districts of Bihar.

2.4 Vision
Vision
To build a just, inclusive, and empowered society where women, children, and
marginalized communities live with dignity, equality, and access to education,
opportunities, and sustainable livelihoods.
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan envisions a community where women are economically
independent, children are protected from exploitation and have access to quality
education, and every individual participates actively in social and economic
development. The organization strives to promote gender equality, social justice, and
sustainable community development through collective action and grassroots
empowerment.
2.5 Mission
Mission
The mission of Mahila Jagriti Sansthan (MJS) is to empower women, protect the rights
of children, and promote inclusive and sustainable development among marginalized
communities. The organization works to strengthen the socio-economic status of
women and disadvantaged groups through education, skill development, awareness
programs, and community participation.
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan is committed to promoting gender equality and addressing
gender-based violence, while ensuring that women and girls live with dignity, safety,
and equal opportunities. Through training, advocacy, legal awareness, and community
mobilization, the organization works to create awareness about women’s rights, prevent
exploitation and discrimination, and encourage social justice at the grassroots level.
By collaborating with communities, government institutions, and partner organizations,
Mahila Jagriti Sansthan strives to build a society where women are empowered,
children are protected, and every individual has the opportunity to live with
dignity, equality, and security.
