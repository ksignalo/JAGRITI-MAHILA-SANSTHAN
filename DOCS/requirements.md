### Founder' Story:
The story of **Mahila Jagriti Sansthan** is deeply connected with the inspiring journey of its founder, **Mrs. Indira Kumari**, a dedicated social activist who has devoted more than **three decades of her life to social service, women empowerment, and child rights protection**.

Born and raised in Bihar, Mrs. Indira Kumari strongly believed that **education and empowerment are the most powerful tools to bring social change**. After completing her **Matriculation from Basudev Mishr High School, Darbhanga in 1984**, she pursued **Intermediate from M.R.M College, Darbhanga in 1986**, followed by her **Bachelor’s Degree (Honours) in 1989 and Master’s Degree from Lalit Narayan Mithila University, Darbhanga**.

Her journey in social work began in **1990**, when she started working with a non-governmental organization to address social challenges affecting women and children in rural communities. During this time, she witnessed the harsh realities of **child labour, lack of education, poverty, and gender inequality**, which deeply motivated her to dedicate her life to social reform.

With a vision to empower women and create opportunities for marginalized communities, she founded **Mahila Jagriti Sansthan in 1993**. Since then, the organization has been actively working at the grassroots level to promote **women empowerment, child education, skill development, and community welfare**.

Under her leadership, Mahila Jagriti Sansthan has implemented several impactful programs including **vocational training for women, self-help group development, child rights awareness campaigns, health camps, environmental awareness programs, and youth skill development initiatives**.

Mrs. Indira Kumari has also played a significant role in **child protection and justice systems**. She served as a **Member of the Juvenile Justice Board (2012–2018)** and later as a **Member of the Child Welfare Committee (2020–2023)**, where she worked tirelessly to safeguard the rights of vulnerable children.

Through collaboration with organizations such as **Bachpan Bachao Andolan and Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation**, she contributed to the rescue and rehabilitation of **over 3,500 children from child labour, trafficking, and exploitation**.

Her dedication has helped **empower more than 15,000 women** through skill development, awareness programs, and livelihood opportunities, enabling them to become financially independent and socially empowered.

Mrs. Indira Kumari continues to inspire communities with her unwavering commitment to **justice, equality, and human dignity**, making her a true catalyst for social change.

Today, her vision continues to guide Mahila Jagriti Sansthan in building a society where **every woman is empowered, every child is educated, and every community has the opportunity to thrive**.

### About Us

The NGO, established in 1993, has profoundly impacted global communities
through its tireless and dedicated efforts over the past 30 years. By combating
child labor and trafficking, it has safeguarded countless children, ensuring they
can grow, learn, and thrive free from exploitation. Its relentless focus on child
education has broken the cycle of poverty, providing opportunities for future
generations and transforming the lives of countless children.
Moreover, the NGO's unwavering commitment to women's empowerment has
fostered economic independence and gender equality. Through skill training in
areas such as handicrafts, it has enabled women to earn a sustainable income,
thereby transforming their lives and communities. The NGO's comprehensive
approach addresses critical issues such as rural development, healthcare, and
disaster management, improving access to essential services and infrastructure.
Its strong advocacy for human rights and cultural development has promoted
inclusivity, peace, and mutual understanding, laying the groundwork for a
harmonious society.
Overall, the NGO's tireless work has not only changed individual lives but also
inspired broader movements for social justice and human dignity. For the last
30 years, it has been working tirelessly for women's empowerment and child
education, continuing to play a vital role in creating a more just, equitable, and
compassionate world.

### Mission

The NGO is dedicated to preventing child labor and trafficking by advocating for and
implementing measures to eliminate these practices, ensuring children's safety and rights
are protected globally. It promotes child education by establishing schools, providing
scholarships, and developing educational programs to break the cycle of poverty and
ignorance, thereby opening doors of opportunity for future generations. The organization
empowers women by providing skill training in areas such as handicrafts, fostering
economic independence, and promoting gender equality, enabling women to become
agents of change in their communities. It enhances rural development by improving
access to basic services and infrastructure, addressing the root causes of social and
economic disparities. To improve healthcare access, the NGO implements programs to
combat diseases such as HIV/AIDS, ensuring communities have essential health services.
It advocates for human rights, peace, and cooperation through advocacy, community
mobilization, and grassroots organizing, amplifying the voices of marginalized and
vulnerable populations. The organization fosters cultural development by celebrating
diversity and promoting mutual understanding through various initiatives, creating an
inclusive and harmonious society. It promotes comprehensive development by addressing
interconnected social issues through initiatives in human resource development, SCs &
STs welfare, agriculture promotion, disaster management, and youth affairs, ensuring no
one is left behind. Through innovative approaches and tireless advocacy, the NGO
inspires and supports movements for social justice and human dignity globally. Finally, it
maintains a holistic approach to development, ensuring that its efforts lead to long-term,
sustainable improvements in communities worldwide.
